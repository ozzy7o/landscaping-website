GROWING PLANTS 101:

Growing your own plants can be one of the most rewarding experiences if you have a green thumbâ€¦ or even a black thumb. This guide will get you started with giving your plants the best start, to allow them to blossom to their full potential.

  • Ageratum houstonianum 'Artist Blue'/Artist Blue Ageratum

    Cool season annual in the south, while it flowers in late spring and summer further north. In the South it has two flowering periods, one in spring and again in late fall when cooler temperatures arrive.

  • Crocosmia 'Lucifer' / Lucifer Montbretia

    This cousin of the gladiolus should be divided in the fall every 3 years. This particular cultivar is an especially good one for the garden. Several others are available in the trade. Flowering is best in full sunlight. Lot of foliage produced in shaded gardens, but blooming will be sparse. Foliage can become untidy in late summer. Cut back in fall.

  • Allium giganteum/Flowering Onion

    Large spheres of purple flowers in spring and summer make good dried material. Provide full sunlight and a well drained soil for the onions. Well suited for plantings in raised beds like planters.
